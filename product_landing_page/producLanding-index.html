Mahogany: Mahogany is a dense wood and gives a “slower response rate” – don’t ask me what that means in scientific terms! But what it does mean in practical terms is that it produces a strong/meaty mid range sound with more subtle overtones (a very “non-abrasive” sound) which leads to an “earthy” “mid-rangy” sound. Mahogany tops are great for playing blues. They are also great in situation where you are playing with other instruments. The strong sound produced is easier to hear in a multi instrumental environment. Whilst it is more commonly used in the back and sides (see below) it also makes for a great top if it suits what you are playing.
